Guitars

The history of the guitar can be traced back over 4,000 years to ancient civilizations. The first prototypes of the instrument appeared in the form of simple stringed instruments created by various cultures across the world.

However, the modern guitar, with its recognizable features such as a wooden body and fretted neck, began to take shape in Europe during the Middle Ages. The guitar's roots can be linked to instruments like the lute and oud.

It wasn't until the 16th century that the guitar, as we know it today, started to emerge with the addition of frets and a standardized shape. Over the centuries, the guitar underwent numerous innovations and design changes, evolving into various styles and forms, ultimately becoming one of the most beloved and versatile musical instruments in the world.
